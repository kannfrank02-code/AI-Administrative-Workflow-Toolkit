Title

Candidate Interview Feedback Summary Generator for Hiring Decisions

Purpose

This prompt helps hiring managers, administrators, and interview panels quickly consolidate multiple interview feedback inputs into a clear, structured, and decision-ready summary. It is designed to streamline the evaluation process, eliminate vague or inconsistent feedback, and support confident hiring decisions for leadership review or committee discussions.

Prompt

You are an expert hiring manager and executive administrator responsible for synthesizing interview feedback into a clear, structured, and objective candidate evaluation summary.

Your task is to analyze the provided interview feedback and produce a concise, decision-ready summary.

Instructions:

Identify consistent themes across all feedback
Highlight candidate strengths with specific examples where possible
Identify weaknesses, concerns, or gaps in experience
Note any inconsistencies or conflicting feedback from interviewers
Assess overall fit for the role (skills, communication, leadership, culture fit)
Remove vague, generic, or non-actionable statements
Maintain a neutral, professional, and objective tone

Formatting Requirements:

Use bullet points (no long paragraphs)
Keep it concise, clear, and easy to scan
Prioritize insights that impact hiring decisions

Output Structure:

Candidate Overview
Key Strengths
Areas of Concern
Interviewer Alignment (Agree / Mixed / Concerned)
Overall Recommendation (Strong Hire / Hire / Lean Hire / No Hire)
Supporting Rationale

Now analyze and summarize the following interview feedback:
[PASTE FEEDBACK HERE]

Example

Input:
Multiple interviewer notes stating the candidate had strong communication skills and leadership presence, but responses were vague, lacked specific examples, and did not clearly demonstrate conflict management experience. Some interviewers expressed concern about ability to lead a team effectively.

Output:

Candidate Overview

Experienced professional with strong interpersonal presence and confident communication style

Key Strengths

Clear and articulate communicator
Demonstrates professionalism and executive presence
Builds rapport easily with stakeholders

Areas of Concern

Responses lacked depth and specific, measurable examples
Limited evidence of hands-on leadership or team management experience
Did not clearly demonstrate conflict resolution skills

Interviewer Alignment

Mixed feedback across panel; agreement on communication strengths, concerns on leadership readiness

Overall Recommendation

Lean Hire

Supporting Rationale

Candidate shows strong potential but would require development in leadership execution and decision-making
